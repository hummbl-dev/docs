The EU AI Act's Annex III high-risk provisions are expected to enter enforcement on **December 2, 2027**, per the May 2026 provisional agreement. If your AI system falls into a high-risk category — recruitment tools, credit scoring, biometric identification, critical infrastructure management, law enforcement applications, and more — you need documented, verifiable runtime controls in place before that date. Non-compliance carries penalties up to **€15 million or 3% of global annual turnover** (Article 99 Tier 2), whichever is higher. The `hummbl-governance` package implements the specific technical controls required by Articles 9 through 14 and produces machine-readable evidence that satisfies what auditors actually ask to see.

<Warning>
  Hummbl provides governance infrastructure, not legal advice. Whether your specific AI system qualifies as high-risk under Annex III is a legal determination. Engage qualified EU AI Act counsel to confirm your classification and obligations before relying solely on technical controls.
</Warning>

## Article Coverage

The six articles that define technical requirements for high-risk AI systems under the EU AI Act each map to one or more Hummbl primitives. The table below shows the mapping and explains how each primitive satisfies the requirement at runtime.

| Article | Requirement | Hummbl Primitive | How |
|---------|-------------|-----------------|-----|
| Art. 9 | Risk management system | Kill Switch + Circuit Breaker | Four-mode halt (`HALT_NONCRITICAL` → `HALT_ALL` → `EMERGENCY`) plus per-adapter failure isolation |
| Art. 10 | Data governance | Governance Bus | Append-only audit trail records every action with a timestamp and agent identity |
| Art. 11 | Technical documentation | All primitives | Every governance event is machine-readable JSONL — exportable as technical documentation artifacts |
| Art. 12 | Record-keeping / logging | Governance Bus (BusWriter) | Timestamped, HMAC-signed log entries that satisfy immutability and traceability requirements |
| Art. 13 | Transparency | Delegation Tokens | Cryptographic attribution ties each action to a specific agent, scope, and authority grant |
| Art. 14 | Human oversight | Kill Switch | Non-delegable halt authority; no agent can suppress or bypass a kill switch invocation |

### Article 12 in Detail: Logging

Article 12 requires that high-risk AI systems automatically generate logs sufficient to assess conformity and identify risks throughout the system's operational lifetime. A log entry produced by `BusWriter` satisfies this requirement by including:

- **Timestamp** — ISO 8601, UTC, millisecond precision
- **Agent identity** — which agent produced the event
- **Event type** — decision, action, policy check, halt, delegation
- **Payload hash** — SHA-256 of the event body
- **HMAC signature** — verifiable chain of custody using your signing key

Every entry is appended to a local JSONL file that an auditor can open, grep, and verify without any Hummbl tooling installed.

```python
from hummbl_governance import BusWriter, BusEvent

writer = BusWriter(log_path="./governance.jsonl", signing_key=YOUR_SIGNING_KEY)

# This single call satisfies Article 12 logging for one agent action
writer.post(
    BusEvent(
        agent_id="loan-underwriting-agent-v2",
        event_type="decision",
        payload={
            "action": "approve_loan_application",
            "application_id": "APP-2024-98731",
            "confidence": 0.87,
            "policy_version": "underwriting-v4.2",
        },
    )
)
```

### Article 14 in Detail: Human Oversight

Article 14 requires that high-risk AI systems be designed and developed such that natural persons can effectively oversee and intervene during the period of use. The Kill Switch implements this requirement with four graduated escalation modes:

| Mode | Effect |
|------|--------|
| `HALT_NONCRITICAL` | Pauses non-critical agent operations; critical paths continue |
| `HALT_CRITICAL` | Pauses all agent operations including critical paths |
| `HALT_ALL` | Stops all operations system-wide |
| `EMERGENCY` | Immediate termination; no graceful shutdown |

Crucially, **no agent can override a kill switch check**. The check is synchronous and runs in the agent's own thread — an agent cannot continue executing after a halt is issued. This satisfies the Article 14 requirement that human oversight measures cannot be circumvented by the AI system itself.

## Quick Compliance Check

Before using the full 12-question assessment, answer these five questions to identify your highest-priority gaps:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Does your system log every decision it makes?">
    Article 12 requires automatic, append-only logging of all AI-generated outputs and the inputs that produced them. If your system only logs errors, you have a gap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Can a human halt your system within two seconds?">
    Article 14 requires that a human can intervene and stop the system during operation. If halt requires a deployment, a support ticket, or cloud console access, you have a gap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Can you trace any output back to the agent and data that produced it?">
    Article 13 requires that the system's logic and decisions be explainable to affected parties. If you cannot reconstruct what happened from your logs, you have a gap.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Does your system have documented risk management procedures that execute at runtime?">
    Article 9 requires a continuous risk management system — not a one-time document. Static policy PDFs do not satisfy this requirement.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Can you export your governance evidence without accessing a vendor's system?">
    Auditors cannot access SaaS dashboards, third-party portals, or vendor-controlled infrastructure during an assessment. If your evidence chain depends on a vendor's availability, you have a portability gap.
  </Step>
</Steps>
