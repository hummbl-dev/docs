Compliance with AI governance frameworks is not a documentation exercise — it is a runtime enforcement problem. Regulators, auditors, and procurement reviewers do not want to read your policy PDFs; they want to see evidence that controls actually executed at the moment decisions were made. Hummbl's `hummbl-governance` package embeds those controls directly into your agent's execution path, producing verifiable, portable evidence as a natural byproduct of normal operation.

## The Portability Argument

When a DIBCAC assessor, HIPAA auditor, or SOC 2 reviewer evaluates your AI system, they need to verify your evidence chain — not trust a vendor dashboard.

| Evidence lives in… | Assessor can… |
|--------------------|---------------|
| Proprietary SaaS dashboard | View screenshots you provide. Cannot run it, grep it, or verify chain of custody. |
| Local JSONL audit log (Hummbl) | Open it in a text editor, grep for specific events, verify HMAC signatures, take a portable copy. |

Because `hummbl-governance` is a stdlib-only Python library (Apache 2.0), it deploys anywhere your code runs: IL4/IL5 enclaves, air-gapped HIPAA environments, regulated cloud perimeters, and on-premises infrastructure that cannot accept outbound SaaS connections. Your governance evidence stays inside your security boundary.

### How Each Primitive Addresses Compliance

**Governance Bus → Audit Trail requirements**
Every action an agent takes is posted to an append-only, HMAC-signed JSONL log. This satisfies EU AI Act Article 12 logging requirements, ISO 42001 incident recording and traceability, NIST AI RMF Manage function documentation, and the availability and processing integrity criteria for SOC 2.

**Kill Switch → Human Oversight requirements**
A non-delegable halt authority with four escalation modes (`HALT_NONCRITICAL`, `HALT_CRITICAL`, `HALT_ALL`, `EMERGENCY`). Satisfies EU AI Act Article 14 human oversight measures, NIST AI RMF Govern function human control mechanisms, and ISO 42001 operational control.

**Delegation Tokens → Accountability requirements**
Cryptographic attribution that ties every action back to a specific agent, scope, and authority grant. Satisfies EU AI Act Article 13 transparency requirements, NIST AI RMF Map function risk identification and attribution, and ISO 42001 roles and responsibilities.

**CapabilityFence → Singapore Agentic AI Framework**
Scope-narrowing enforcement on delegation chains prevents privilege escalation between agent handoffs. Directly addresses Singapore IMDA delegation chain depth limits and identity verification requirements.

**Circuit Breaker → Risk Management requirements**
Per-adapter failure rate tracking with automatic isolation. Satisfies NIST AI RMF Measure and Manage functions and ISO 42001 risk treatment.

## Air-Gap and Regulated Environment Support

`hummbl-governance` has zero external dependencies — it is pure Python standard library. This means you can deploy it in environments where outbound internet access is prohibited or where third-party SaaS tooling is not permitted:

- **IL4 / IL5 government enclaves** — no dependency on external package mirrors after initial install
- **Air-gapped HIPAA environments** — audit logs stay on-premises and never leave your security boundary
- **Regulated financial infrastructure** — no SaaS data residency concerns; logs live wherever you run your code
- **FedRAMP-bounded systems** — stdlib-only means no unapproved external services

If your compliance obligation requires that evidence never leave a specific network perimeter, a library is the only architectural choice that satisfies that requirement.
