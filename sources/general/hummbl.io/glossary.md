# Glossary

Canonical definitions for HUMMBL's core terms: the frozen reasoning kernel, the proprietary lattice framework, the derived domain lattices, the epistemic breadth stance, and the truth/utility operating standard.

## Core terms

[Base120](https://hummbl.io/base120.html) Frozen

HUMMBL's governed reasoning substrate: 120 canonical mental-model operators organized across six transformation families (Perspective, Inversion, Composition, Decomposition, Recursion, Systems). Frozen at v1.0.0 as an open, immutable canonical kernel.

[BaseN](https://hummbl.io/basen.html) Proprietary

HUMMBL's proprietary framework for generating, validating, and governing domain-specific reasoning lattices. Generated Domain120 candidates are promoted through a structured review process before becoming ratified sets.

[Domain120](https://hummbl.io/domain120.html)

Field-specific ratified 6×20 lattices derived from the Base120 architecture. Generated by BaseN, curated by domain experts, validated by cross-domain stress tests, and ratified by governance.

[HUAOMP](https://hummbl.io/huaomp.html)

Holistic, Universal, Absolute, Omni, Meta, Paradigmatic — a six-lens orientation for maximum epistemic breadth when scoping intent and expanding problems before narrowing to action.

[MTSMU](https://hummbl.io/mtsmu.html)

Maximally Truth-Seeking, Maximally Useful — the HUMMBL standard for evidence-first outputs that are both accurate and actionable. Used to rank HUAOMP combinations and drive the automation loop.

## How the terms relate

[Base120](https://hummbl.io/base120.html) is the frozen operator catalog. [HUAOMP](https://hummbl.io/huaomp.html) is the epistemic stance for selecting which lenses to apply. [MTSMU](https://hummbl.io/mtsmu.html) is the standard for judging whether the resulting output is both true and useful. [BaseN](https://hummbl.io/basen.html) is the engine that generates [Domain120](https://hummbl.io/domain120.html) candidates from the Base120 architecture, subject to MTSMU validation and governance promotion.

For the full architecture diagram, see the [HUMMBL System Map](https://hummbl.io/system-map.html).
